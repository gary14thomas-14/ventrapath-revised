# VentraPath Benchmark Lanes

## Why split the benchmark

VentraPath is doing two different jobs:

1. **Company blueprint generation**
   - Take a commercially legible idea and turn it into a strong, UI-fit, momentum-building blueprint.

2. **Messy idea rescue / rejection**
   - Take a fuzzy, half-formed, commercially risky idea and either narrow it honestly or reject it cleanly.

Scoring both jobs as one blended pass rate muddies the signal.

## Lane A — Blueprint generation

### Goal

Measure whether VentraPath can produce a compelling Phase 0 blueprint when the starting idea already has a believable business spine.

### Pass criteria

A case passes when the output:

- identifies one clear buyer
- identifies one clear payer
- commits to one clear business form
- surfaces a visible differentiating twist early
- fits the VentraPath UI structure
- opens with a short, punchy business brief
- feels commercially legible, not generic

### Fail criteria

A case fails when the output:

- drifts into generic category fluff
- blurs business forms together
- buries or misses the twist
- feels flat, bloated, or non-UI-shaped
- needs major Bob rescue to become coherent

## Lane B — Messy idea rescue / rejection

### Goal

Measure whether VentraPath handles fuzzy free-text input honestly.

### Pass criteria

A case passes when VentraPath does **either** of these well:

1. **Honest rescue**
   - finds a strongest commercially credible interpretation
   - does not invent a different business
   - produces a legible conditional pass or pass

2. **Clean rejection**
   - identifies the real blocker clearly
   - rejects for the right commercial reason
   - avoids fake clarity and fake monetisation

### Fail criteria

A case fails when the system:

- fabricates a buyer, payer, or business form
- invents an operating spine the input did not earn
- produces commercially vague mush
- rejects for the wrong reason while missing the real issue

## Recommended reporting

Track the lanes separately:

- Blueprint lane: pass / conditional pass / fail
- Messy lane: honest rescue / clean rejection / bad rescue / muddled failure

The goal is not to make both lanes look identical.
