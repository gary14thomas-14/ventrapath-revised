# Bob Synthesis Benchmark Cases

These cases isolate Bob's final synthesis step using already-generated specialist outputs.

## Standard synthesis task

Bob should merge the supplied specialist outputs into the standard Phase 0 shape:

1. Business
2. Market
3. Monetisation
4. Execution
5. Legal
6. Website
7. Risks

Rules:
- keep it concise and commercially sharp
- do not expose raw specialist fragments
- preserve the unique twist

## Case BS-001 / VP-P0-001-RERUN

### User idea
Australia — subscription-based platform helping busy dog owners book vetted dog walkers, pet sitters, and short-term home check-ins locally.

### Specialist outputs to synthesize

- Blueprint Architect:
  - Business is an Australian subscription-led local pet-care platform for busy dog owners.
  - Supply is vetted independent carers; demand is urban/suburban owners with repeat care needs.
  - Value is trusted local care, continuity, simple rebooking, and reduced admin burden.
  - Website should be trust-first, convenience-first, and explain services, vetting, pricing, and local availability.

- Differentiation Strategist:
  - Recommended twist: Dog Routine Guarantee.
  - Product promise: every caregiver follows saved routine data like walk style, home entry rules, feeding/meds notes, and update format.
  - Generic marketplace positioning is high-risk and unacceptable.

- Market Intelligence:
  - Strongest audience is busy metro / inner-suburban Australian dog owners with disposable income and repeat care needs.
  - Opportunity is trust + repeat convenience.
  - Main risk is local supply density and inconsistent service quality.

- Monetisation Architect:
  - Best shape is low-friction membership plus per-booking commission.
  - Membership likely works only if it clearly reduces friction, improves reliability, and gives real repeat-use value.
  - Biggest risk is subscription friction and double-charging perception.

## Case BS-002 / VP-P0-002

### User idea
United Kingdom — service that helps small trades businesses turn WhatsApp conversations into booked jobs, quotes, reminders, and follow-ups.

### Specialist outputs to synthesize

- Blueprint Architect:
  - Business is a UK SaaS + light-service platform for small trades firms.
  - It turns messy WhatsApp lead flow into structured job workflow: lead capture, quote prep, booking coordination, reminders, and follow-up.
  - Core buyers are owner-operators and small teams.
  - Website should lead with “Turn WhatsApp enquiries into booked jobs.”

- Differentiation Strategist:
  - Recommended twist: UK trades WhatsApp job desk.
  - It should feel lighter than a CRM, more operational than a chatbot, and built for micro-teams already living in chat.
  - Broad SMB automation framing is too generic.

- Market Intelligence:
  - Best audience is UK small trade firms too small for heavy field-service software but big enough to feel admin pain.
  - Market wedge is booked-job ROI, not AI novelty.
  - Main risk is uneven WhatsApp usage across trades.

- Monetisation Architect:
  - Best model is tiered SaaS priced on admin time saved and extra jobs booked.
  - Plausible price bands: sole trader, small team, larger operator tiers.
  - Main risks are setup friction, support-heavy customers, and platform dependency.

## Case BS-003 / VP-P0-003

### User idea
Canada — vague early idea around wellness, AI, and community for burned-out people; unclear whether the business should be an app, coaching business, membership, or service.

### Specialist outputs to synthesize

- Blueprint Architect:
  - Strongest shape is membership-first service, not app-first.
  - Offer combines AI-guided check-ins, structured burnout support tracks, peer community, and light human coaching.
  - Website should position this as burnout recovery support, not therapy, and push to waitlist/pilot.

- Differentiation Strategist:
  - Recommended twist: match members by burnout type, work context, and recovery stage.
  - Generic AI wellness community framing is weak.
  - Bob should narrow the first audience hard.

- Market Intelligence:
  - Real pain exists among Canadian burned-out professionals, especially knowledge workers and other high-pressure roles.
  - Best market shape is hybrid membership/service rather than pure app.
  - Major risks are trust, privacy, clinical boundary confusion, and vague outcomes.

- Monetisation Architect:
  - Best model is low-cost membership plus group/cohort upsells and selective premium coaching.
  - App-first monetisation is the weakest path.
  - Biggest risks are churn, moderation costs, and weak willingness to pay for generic AI wellness.
