# VentraPath Delta Batch B Results

## Scope

Re-test of 3 ambiguity-heavy failures using the current rules, including the new forcing gate for:
- one primary buyer
- one primary payer
- one primary business form

Method used per case:
1. Blueprint Architect pass
2. Differentiation Strategist pass
3. Market Intelligence pass
4. Monetisation Architect pass
5. Bob synthesis only if specialist output was usable

## Results

### VP-DELTA-B-001 (was VP-P0-024 / VP-DELTA-002)

- previous result: Fail
- new result: Conditional pass
- changed?: yes
- short reason: The forcing gate finally turns this from vague support-category fog into a concrete consumer membership: a mother-paid family-load management service. It is still fragile and crowded, but it is no longer commercially shapeless.
- notable effect of buyer/payer/business-form forcing: Real improvement. Forcing one buyer, one payer, and one form killed the drift between app, support group, and service and made the strongest interpretation hold.

**Specialist passes**
- Blueprint Architect: Usable — strongest shape is a mother-paid membership focused on household-load coordination and weekly control, not a generic community.
- Differentiation Strategist: Usable — the best twist is a family-load ledger that turns invisible household work into visible delegation, routines, and accountability.
- Market Intelligence: Mixed but usable — the pain is real and recurring, but acquisition and retention will be hard in a crowded parenting/wellness category.
- Monetisation Architect: Usable — membership plus optional concierge or coaching upsell is coherent once the mother is clearly the payer.
- Specialist usability verdict: Usable

**Current Bob synthesis verdict**
- Bob can now produce a commercially legible version: a membership for overwhelmed mothers that combines AI planning, household workload visibility, and accountability to help them regain control of family operations.
- This is still only a conditional pass because consumer willingness to pay, emotional positioning, and category crowding remain serious risks.
- Commercial read: materially better than the previous fail. Not amazing, but now real.

### VP-DELTA-B-002 (was VP-P0-025 / VP-DELTA-003)

- previous result: Fail
- new result: Fail
- changed?: no
- short reason: The gate improves discipline, but the business still does not pick a truly convincing commercial center. Parent-paid pathway planning is the least-bad version, but it still feels like a forced narrowing of a generic guidance concept.
- notable effect of buyer/payer/business-form forcing: Mostly wording improvement. It forces a cleaner parent-payer frame, but it does not create a strong enough wedge to escape school-tool / coaching / mentor-platform sprawl.

**Specialist passes**
- Blueprint Architect: Mixed — strongest shape is a parent-paid guidance service for older secondary students, but the product boundary still feels unstable.
- Differentiation Strategist: Weak — AI plus mentors plus community still sounds generic, and the twist never becomes strong enough.
- Market Intelligence: Usable — parent anxiety and spend are plausible, but the buyer-user split is still awkward and crowded.
- Monetisation Architect: Mixed — parent payment is the best forced answer, but the model still feels more chosen than earned.
- Specialist usability verdict: Not usable

**Bob synthesis verdict**
- Not run. The specialist layer still does not produce a sufficiently sharp, defensible business.
- Commercial read: cleaner framing, same failure.

### VP-DELTA-B-003 (was VP-P0-032)

- previous result: Fail
- new result: Fail
- changed?: no
- short reason: The forcing gate exposes the real problem instead of solving it. Any clean buyer/payer choice here starts to invent a different business from the one the input actually describes.
- notable effect of buyer/payer/business-form forcing: No real improvement. It pressures the case into fake clarity — NGO programme, employer-paid placement service, or youth subscription — but none of those feel honestly grounded in the input.

**Specialist passes**
- Blueprint Architect: Weak — the cleanest shapes require picking an employer-placement service or sponsored programme, which is too big a leap from the starting idea.
- Differentiation Strategist: Weak — AI, peer support, and training remain generic unless the business commits to placement execution, and the input does not.
- Market Intelligence: Mixed — youth employment pain is obvious, but the actual customer and budget owner are still unresolved.
- Monetisation Architect: Weak — every monetisation route depends on choosing a payer the concept has not earned.
- Specialist usability verdict: Not usable

**Bob synthesis verdict**
- Not run. The case still fails.
- Commercial read: the new gate is useful here as a rejection tool, not a rescue tool.

## Batch summary

- improved cases count: 1
- unchanged cases count: 2
- worsened cases count: 0
- whether the new gate solved real ambiguity or only improved wording: It solved real ambiguity once, on VP-DELTA-B-001, by locking the concept into a credible paid membership instead of a fuzzy blend of app/community/service. On the other two, it mostly improved wording or exposed that any forced choice would be artificial rather than commercially honest.

## Bottom line

The new gate helps when there is a real strongest interpretation hiding inside the mess.

It does not help when the input still lacks a believable payer or when forcing business form means inventing a different business.

Real improvement:
- VP-DELTA-B-001

No material change:
- VP-DELTA-B-002
- VP-DELTA-B-003
