## Results

### VP-P0-012 — India — lightweight hiring workflow for small agencies using WhatsApp

- Specialist phase: Usable
- Specialist ceiling/runtime observation: Not instrumented in this run
- Bob synthesis runtime: Not instrumented in this run
- Pass/fail: Pass
- Reason: Strong agency-side workflow pain with clear recurring usage, visible ROI, and a real twist once the concept is framed as a WhatsApp-to-placement control layer rather than generic recruiting software.
- Commercial notes:
  - Blueprint held up best as a small-agency candidate-flow desk, not a full ATS.
  - Best twist was a shared candidate-thread ledger that converts scattered WhatsApp conversations into stage tracking, interview holds, follow-up prompts, and recruiter accountability.
  - Market logic was credible because many smaller agencies in India already run candidate communication in chat-heavy workflows and lose placements through admin leakage rather than demand shortage.
  - Monetisation worked through per-recruiter or per-agency SaaS with optional placement-linked tiers; the main risk is feature creep into broad HR software.

### VP-P0-013 — South Africa — vehicle servicing, repair quotes, pickup logistics, and reminders membership

- Specialist phase: Usable
- Specialist ceiling/runtime observation: Not instrumented in this run
- Bob synthesis runtime: Not instrumented in this run
- Pass/fail: Pass with intervention required
- Reason: Real consumer pain exists, but the business only stays credible when narrowed to a vehicle-care continuity membership that standardises quotes and coordinates logistics rather than sounding like a vague car concierge.
- Commercial notes:

  - Monetisation worked best as membership plus partner-service margin, but trust, supply quality, and operational intensity keep this below a clean pass.

### VP-P0-022 — Germany — apprentice practice tracking, coach feedback, and certification milestones

- Specialist phase: Usable
- Specialist ceiling/runtime observation: Not instrumented in this run
- Bob synthesis runtime: Not instrumented in this run
- Pass/fail: Pass
- Reason: Strong training workflow concept once focused on assessment readiness and coach sign-off rather than generic learning support; the certification spine gives the product a real commercial center.
- Commercial notes:
  - Blueprint was strongest as an apprenticeship progress and evidence system for skilled trades, not a general education platform.

  - Monetisation worked through school, training-provider, or employer-paid seats, with the main risk being long sales cycles if the product aims too high into institutional software replacement.

## Batch 5 summary

- Cases completed: 6

- Notable recurring failure modes:
  - Wellness, community, and concierge ideas that never harden into a proprietary workflow or buyer-specific operational job.
  - Inputs with unresolved payer logic or unclear software-versus-service boundaries.
  - Consumer concepts that only become viable after aggressive narrowing around continuity, records, or coordination, and still often remain fragile.
- Production-readiness effect:
  - Batch 5 modestly improved confidence for operational SMB and training workflow cases where the system could anchor on clear admin pain and a defensible ledger-like twist.
  - Batch 5 weakened confidence again on vague community, wellness, and travel-family inputs; those still collapse unless the starting idea is forced into a far tighter business definition.
  - Net read: Batch 5 slightly weakened overall confidence in production readiness because the hot path still fails too often on messy consumer/support concepts.
