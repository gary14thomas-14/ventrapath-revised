# Hook Test Results

## Method

Default Phase 0 hot path tested conceptually against the current prompts:
- Blueprint Architect
- Differentiation Strategist
- Market Intelligence
- Monetisation Architect
- Bob synthesis

Focus: hook strength, twist visibility, UI fit, brevity/momentum, and whether the output pulls the user forward.

## Results

### HT-001
- verdict: strong
- what hooks well: The WhatsApp-first trades job desk lands fast. "Turn chat into booked jobs" is immediate, commercial, and easy to picture. The twist is visible early when the workflow becomes a booked-job control layer instead of vague SMB automation.
- what feels flat: Very little. The only mild drag is that it can slip toward generic workflow language if Bob lets the opening get too software-y.
- should prompts change? no

### HT-002
- verdict: acceptable
- what hooks well: The single action queue for support, fulfilment, and exception handling is a real twist, and it fits the VentraPath UI well because the sections naturally collapse into one operating spine.
- what feels flat: It is commercially solid but less naturally punchy than HT-001. "Lightweight operations layer" is accurate, but not inherently exciting. The hook depends on Bob keeping the wording sharp and owner-facing.
- should prompts change? no

### HT-003
- verdict: strong
- what hooks well: The cancellation-to-fill engine is a good hook. It makes the clinic idea feel like revenue recovery and schedule rescue, not reminder software. The ROI is obvious and the twist shows up early enough.
- what feels flat: If the brief leans too hard on reminders and rebooking prompts before the fill-engine angle, the energy drops. That is a wording discipline issue, not a prompt gap.
- should prompts change? no

### HT-004
- verdict: weak
- what hooks well: Almost nothing in the raw idea. "AI ice cream store in New York" is generic, novelty-first, and commercially blurry.
- what feels flat: No earned operating spine, no credible buyer-side wedge, no real twist beyond "AI", and no reason this deserves a VentraPath blueprint instead of immediate rejection. If Bob is strict, this should fail rather than get dressed up.
- should prompts change? no

## Batch Summary

- overall hook quality verdict: strong enough overall
- whether the system is generally attention-grabbing enough: Yes. On commercially real inputs with an actual operating spine, the system is already producing concise, UI-fit, forward-pulling blueprint shapes. The hooks are strongest when the twist is operational, not branding fluff.
- whether another prompt change is justified or unnecessary: Unnecessary right now. The weak case is weak because the input is weak. That is not a prompt failure. Do not keep tuning just to rescue junk ideas.

## Bottom line

The current prompt stack is already doing the right thing:
- strong hook quality on good B2B workflow ideas
- acceptable-to-strong pull-forward on solid admin/ops concepts
- correct failure pressure on generic nonsense

That is the behavior you want. Do not over-optimize past this without a new pattern of failures.
